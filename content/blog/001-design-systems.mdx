# The Art of Design Systems

In the world of frontend development, consistency is king. A well-crafted design system isn't just about making things look prettyâ€”it's about creating a shared language between designers and developers.

## Why You Need One

<Image src="https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1558655146-9f40138edfeb?w=800&q=80" alt="UI design and components" width={800} height={500} />

When you're building a small project, you can get away with hardcoding values. `margin-top: 20px` here, `color: #333` there. But as your application grows, this approach becomes a nightmare.

> "A design system is a collection of reusable components, guided by clear standards, that can be assembled together to build any number of applications."

## Key Components

1. **Design Tokens**: The smallest atoms of your system (colors, spacing, typography).
2. **Component Library**: Reusable UI elements (buttons, inputs, modals).
3. **Documentation**: Usage guidelines and best practices.
